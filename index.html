Where & when I work

  • Based in Cape Town, South Africa.
  • Core hours are 08:30 – 17:30 (SAST).
  • My day starts at 06:30 with a school run, then the gym until 08:15. I'm at my desk by 08:30, catching up on messages over breakfast, with deep work starting at 09:00.
  • I'm offline by 17:30, that's dinner and family time.
  • I don't check Slack / email / Teams outside working hours unless it's planned and temporary (e.g. a critical go-live).

Please don't schedule over my lunch break. I take an hour to fetch my kid from school. The slot varies by day and is always on my calendar. Being the last one in the schoolyard, wondering if Dad forgot, is not a great feeling.

💬

Communication

  • Get straight to the point. Greeting plus what you need, all in one message. No "hi… [waits]", please.
  • I've been told my expression is very serious. I promise I'm not!
  • Always happy to jump on a 1:1 call, work or non-work. I prefer video on (talking to a profile picture feels odd), but no stress if it’s not an option for you.
  • In chats and long discussions I often go quiet to think. That's not disinterest, my contribution is still loading.
  • For the same reason, surprise questions in large meetings aren't where I shine. A quick heads-up and I'll come prepared.
  • Meetings I'm expected to actively participate in should have an agenda.
  • Feedback is welcome. 1:1s or DM works best.
🤝

What you can expect from me

  • Direct, honest answers. I'll tell you what I think, not what's comfortable.
  • Considered responses over fast ones. If I say "let me think about it", I will come back to you.
  • I'll flag early if something is at risk, rather than surprise you late.

Outside work

You'll find me rock climbing (City Rock or outdoors), mountain biking, trail running, hiking, freediving / snorkelling, kickboxing, gaming, reading (hardcopy books) or travelling. Happy to talk about any of these, for far longer than you'd like.

My dislikes

  • Being micromanaged.
  • Being interrupted constantly, especially mid-answer to a question I was asked.
  • Performative listening: waiting for your turn to speak instead of actually hearing me out.
  • Being patronised or treated condescendingly.
